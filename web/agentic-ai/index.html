What is Agentic AI?

Imagine having a highly capable assistant who doesn't just answer your questions but can actually take action on your behalf. That's the essence of agentic AI. Unlike traditional AI that primarily responds to commands or generates content, agentic AI can set goals, make decisions, and execute tasks with minimal human intervention.

Think of it this way: regular AI is like a knowledgeable librarian who can find information for you. Agentic AI is like a skilled project manager who not only finds information but also organizes it, creates plans, and carries out tasks to achieve specific objectives.

Agentic AI represents a significant leap forward in artificial intelligence. These systems can reason through complex problems, use various tools and applications, and learn from their experiences to improve over time. They operate with a higher degree of autonomy, making them incredibly valuable for businesses and individuals alike.

The technology is built on advanced components including perception (gathering information), reasoning (analyzing data), planning (developing strategies), action (executing tasks), and reflection (learning from results). This continuous cycle allows agentic AI to handle multi-step tasks that previously required extensive human oversight.

What makes agentic AI truly revolutionary is its ability to coordinate multiple specialized agents working together. Just as a successful company relies on different departments with distinct skills, agentic AI systems can deploy various agents‚Äîeach specializing in specific tasks‚Äîto accomplish complex objectives efficiently and effectively.

How Agentic AI Works

Agentic AI operates through a sophisticated process that mimics human problem-solving. Let's break it down into five simple steps:

1

Perception

The AI gathers information from various sources like databases, documents, emails, or sensors. It analyzes this data to understand the situation and identify what needs to be done.

2

Reasoning

Using advanced language models, the AI analyzes the gathered information to understand context, identify relevant details, and figure out potential solutions to the problem.

3

Planning

The AI develops a clear plan by setting specific goals, breaking them down into smaller steps, and determining the best approach to achieve them efficiently.

4

Action

Based on its plan, the AI takes action by performing tasks, making decisions, or interacting with other systems and applications to move toward the goal.

5

Reflection

After taking action, the AI learns from the results, evaluates whether its actions were successful, and uses this feedback to improve its future performance.

This continuous cycle of perception, reasoning, planning, action, and reflection enables agentic AI to handle increasingly complex tasks while continuously improving its capabilities.

Real-World Company Implementations

Leading companies across industries are already deploying agentic AI with impressive results. Here are some notable examples:

Delivery Hero's QueryAnswerBird

Food Delivery Platform

Delivery Hero built an AI-powered data analyst assistant called QueryAnswerBird (QAB) that enables employees to query and visualize business data without writing code. The system combines natural language processing with retrieval-augmented generation to access internal data and generate SQL queries automatically.

Result: Employees can now access business insights instantly through simple questions in Slack, dramatically reducing the time needed for data analysis and improving organizational data literacy.

eBay's Mercury Platform

Online Marketplace

eBay developed Mercury, an internal agentic AI platform that powers AI-driven recommendation experiences on their marketplace. The platform integrates real-time domain-specific data and includes a Listing Matching Engine that converts text suggestions into relevant live listings from eBay's two-billion-item inventory.

Result: The platform enables teams to efficiently build and scale autonomous, goal-oriented AI workflows, improving recommendation accuracy and personalization for millions of users.

Uber's Enhanced Agentic RAG

Transportation Network

Uber implemented Enhanced Agentic RAG (EAg-RAG) to improve answer quality in their on-call copilot called Genie. The system adds AI agents at multiple stages to optimize queries, identify sources, and post-process retrieved context for better accuracy.

Result: The share of acceptable answers increased by 27%, and incorrect advice was reduced by 60% compared to traditional AI architectures, demonstrating the power of multi-agent systems.

Salesforce's Ask Astro

Customer Relationship Management

Salesforce built the Ask Astro agent for their Events mobile app, enabling attendees to ask natural-language questions, get session recommendations, and manage personal schedules. The agent ingests structured event data and unstructured FAQs using hybrid search architecture.

Result: Attendees can easily find relevant sessions and manage their schedules through conversational queries, significantly improving the event experience and reducing the need for manual navigation.

Google's Jules

Technology Giant

Google developed Jules, a massively parallel asynchronous AI coding agent that assists developers by autonomously performing common coding tasks including fixing bugs, writing tests, and updating dependencies. It runs tasks in the background and reports back with completed pull requests.

Result: Developers can focus on creative problem-solving while Jules handles routine coding tasks, running multiple variations in parallel and allowing developers to review or merge the best outcomes.

LinkedIn's Hiring Assistant

Professional Networking Platform

LinkedIn created a modular multi-agent system called Hiring Assistant that automates recruiter workflows. One supervisory agent orchestrates sub-agents that handle discrete functions like drafting job descriptions, sourcing candidates, messaging, and ranking candidates.

Result: Recruiters save significant time on routine tasks while maintaining quality, with each agent instance tied to its own recruiter for personalized assistance throughout the hiring process.

  • Widespread Enterprise Integration: Predictions indicate that 33% of enterprise applications will feature agentic AI capabilities by 2028, up from less than 1% in 2024. This massive integration will make AI-powered automation a standard feature across business software.
  • Enhanced Human-AI Collaboration: Rather than replacing humans, 89% of industry professionals emphasize combining human connection with AI efficiency. This collaborative approach will create new roles and opportunities where humans and AI work together synergistically.
  • Continuous Learning Systems: Next-generation agentic platforms will incorporate reinforcement learning, enabling systems to improve performance autonomously without human intervention. These self-optimizing capabilities will make AI increasingly valuable over time.
  • Multi-Agent Dominance: Currently, 66.4% of the market focuses on coordinated multi-agent systems rather than single-agent solutions. This trend will continue as organizations recognize the power of specialized agents working together to accomplish complex objectives.
  • Industry-Specific Solutions: As the technology matures, we'll see more specialized agentic AI solutions tailored to specific industries, from healthcare and finance to manufacturing and education, each optimized for unique challenges and requirements.
  • Democratization of AI: The adoption timeline continues to shrink, with organizations launching initial agents within 90 days using modern platforms. This accessibility will allow businesses of all sizes to leverage agentic AI capabilities.
